# SciLens — Academic / Basic Research Scoring Guide

## Scope
Fundamental research, theoretical work, non-applied science, literature reviews, hypothesis papers.

## TRL Calibration
Basic research typically maps to TRL 1–3. Do not inflate.
| Paper evidence | TRL |
|---------------|-----|
| Hypothesis, theoretical model | 1 |
| Computational / in silico validation | 2 |
| Experimental proof of concept (lab) | 3 |
| Applied demonstration possible | 4 |

## Evidence Quality Priority
meta_analysis > well-designed experimental > observational > theoretical

## Key Analysis Focus
- Scientific novelty and contribution to field understanding
- Methodological rigor (controls, sample size, reproducibility)
- Translational potential: which applied domain could use this?
- Quality of the journal / peer review signals
- Citation potential and influence on downstream research

## Market Context Instructions
Market validation data may be minimal or absent for basic research — this is expected.
Focus synthesis on: scientific impact, translational opportunities, and what applied domains
could build on this work. Do NOT fabricate market relevance that doesn't exist.

## Risk Factors to Prioritize
1. Long translation horizon (basic → applied is typically 5–15 years)
2. Reproducibility concerns if methods are novel
3. Limited scope (single organism, narrow conditions)
4. Funding/continuation risk for early-stage academic work
