# SciLens — Agro Health Scoring Guide

## Scope
Crop protection, biopesticides, biostimulants, food safety, phytosanitary, livestock, aquaculture, GMO.

## TRL Calibration
| Paper evidence | TRL |
|---------------|-----|
| In vitro bioassay / computational model | 1–2 |
| Greenhouse proof of concept | 3 |
| Controlled field trial, single site | 4–5 |
| Multi-location field trial, dose-response | 6–7 |
| Registered product / commercial validation | 8–9 |

## Evidence Quality Priority (agro context)
Multi-location field trial > single-location field trial > greenhouse > in vivo bioassay > in vitro > in_silico

## Key Analysis Focus
- Efficacy under realistic field conditions vs. controlled lab
- Regulatory approval pathway:
  - EU: EFSA plant protection authorization
  - US: EPA FIFRA (biopesticide) / USDA APHIS (biotech crops)
  - Codex Alimentarius MRL compliance
- Pest resistance risk and management strategy
- Environmental persistence and ecotoxicology data
- Social acceptance for GM crops in target markets

## Market Context Instructions
Agro approvals often depend on country-by-country phytosanitary registration.
Note which markets the paper targets and regulatory complexity per region.

## Risk Factors to Prioritize
1. Regulatory fragmentation (country-by-country registration)
2. Pest resistance development timeline
3. Climate and agronomic condition dependency
4. Social acceptance of GM/synthetic biology approaches
5. Patent landscape for the trait or formulation
