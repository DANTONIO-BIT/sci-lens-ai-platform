# SciLens — Biotech Scoring Guide

## Scope
Biologics, cell/gene therapy, synthetic biology, fermentation, bioprocessing, ATMP.

## TRL Calibration
| Paper evidence | TRL |
|---------------|-----|
| Genetic/protein design, computational | 1–2 |
| In vitro cell-based proof of concept | 3 |
| In vivo (animal) efficacy + safety data | 4 |
| Phase I (safety, first-in-human) | 5 |
| Phase II (efficacy in patients) | 6 |
| Phase III (pivotal trial) | 7–8 |
| Approved biologic / ATMP | 9 |

## Evidence Quality Priority
Clinical data > in_vivo > in_vitro > in_silico

## Key Analysis Focus
- Manufacturability: titer, yield, upstream/downstream scalability
- Immunogenicity and off-target effects
- Regulatory body: FDA CBER (US) / EMA CAT (EU advanced therapies)
- Viral vector production and safety (for gene therapy)
- Biosimilarity and comparability if applicable

## Market Context Instructions
Use provided trial and approval data to assess market maturity.
Note: biotech approvals are harder to achieve than small molecules — weight active trials heavily.

## Risk Factors to Prioritize
1. Manufacturing scale-up complexity
2. Immunogenicity risk for the biologic modality
3. Regulatory precedent (is this modality approved anywhere?)
4. Cold chain / distribution challenges
5. Competitive landscape in the target indication
